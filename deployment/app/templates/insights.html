Feature Engineering Rationale

Why each feature group was added and what it captures

Pollutant 11 features

Raw measurements

PM2.5, PM10, NO, NO2, NOx, NH3, CO, SO2, O3, Benzene, Toluene. Primary physical drivers of AQI. PM2.5 is the single strongest predictor (SHAP rank #1, mean |SHAP| = 40.05).

Lag 12 features

Temporal persistence

PM2.5, NO2, CO, O3 at lag-1, lag-3, lag-7 days. Captures atmospheric persistence - yesterday's PM2.5 is rank #2 (mean |SHAP| = 28.28), reflecting that pollution does not disappear overnight.

Temporal 5 features

Cyclical encoding

Month and day-of-year encoded as sin/cos pairs. Preserves continuity - December and January are adjacent, not 11 months apart. Year added as linear trend to capture gradual air quality improvement.

Rolling 4 features

Momentum statistics

7-day mean and 3-day max for PM2.5 and NO2. Captures sustained pollution episodes vs sudden spikes. The 3-day peak is more informative than the 7-day mean for acute health warning scenarios.

Spatial 1 feature

City context

Target-encoded city mean AQI. Encodes each city's historical pollution baseline as a single numeric feature. Preferred over one-hot encoding for tree models - avoids high cardinality while preserving city identity.
