# 10. External Validation Against CPCB 2024 Data

## Overview

Independent validation using official CPCB daily AQI bulletins for all five cities (2024). This data was collected separately from CPCB portal and was not used in any stage of model training or development. Validates two core claims:

1. Seasonal patterns learned from 2015-2020 persist into 2024 — model generalizes beyond training window
2. SHAP feature importances align with real-world pollutant prominence — model learned genuine atmospheric chemistry

## 10.1 Data Source

- **Source:** Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) official daily AQI bulletins
- **Format:** Wide-format Excel (days as rows, months as columns) — parsed to long format
- **Coverage:** All five study cities, full year 2024 (366 records per city)
- **Content:** Daily AQI values + prominent pollutant flags (Delhi only)

## 10.2 Seasonal Pattern Comparison

Training-era seasonal patterns preserved across all five cities in 2024:

All five cities confirmed **Winter > Monsoon** pattern in both time periods — seasonal learning from 2015-2020 generalizes correctly to 2024.

**Notable city profiles:**

- **Delhi:** Widest seasonal range (134–402 train) — landlocked geography, stubble burning dominates winter
- **Chennai:** Flattest seasonal profile (104–128 train) — coastal climate suppresses winter trapping effect
- **Kolkata:** Sharpest winter spike relative to monsoon — river valley trapping effect similar to Delhi
- **Mumbai:** Consistently lowest absolute values — sea breeze aids year-round dispersion
- **Bangalore:** Low values, moderate variation — high altitude (920m) aids dispersion

## 10.3 Annual Mean AQI Comparison

All cities show consistent AQI reduction in 2024 relative to 2015–2020 training baseline:

**Chennai** shows largest relative improvement (−36.8%) — consistent with coastal dispersion advantages and southern India's faster EV adoption.

**Delhi** shows smallest relative improvement (−19.3%) — consistent with persistent structural emission sources: vehicular density, industrial load, and crop burning.

**City pollution ranking preserved:**
Delhi > Kolkata > Chennai > Mumbai > Bangalore — the model's `city_aqi_mean` feature correctly captures this hierarchy in both time periods.

## 10.4 Prominent Pollutant Validation (Delhi)

CPCB identifies which pollutants drove AQI each day. Compared against SHAP seasonal findings from Section 9.4 as an independent cross-check of model learning.

**5/5 agreement** between SHAP seasonal findings (derived from 2015–2020 training data) and independent CPCB 2024 prominent pollutant measurements confirms the model learned genuine atmospheric chemistry — not statistical artifacts of the training period.

## 10.5 Winter Severity Analysis (Delhi)

Detailed November–December comparison resolves the apparent contradiction between data showing "improvement" and news media reporting "crisis":

**Statistical improvement is real:**
Mean AQI dropped 52.9 μg/m³, worst days no longer reach off-scale values (716 → 494). Consistent with BS6 fuel standards (2020), increased EV adoption, and stricter CPCB enforcement post-2020.

**Experiential reality is also real:**
2024 median November–December AQI = 346 (Very Poor). 75% of winter days exceed 393 (Very Poor/Severe). The improvement from 386 → 333 mean is statistically meaningful but experientially imperceptible — both baselines are uniformly hazardous. This explains why news media continues to report a pollution crisis despite measured improvement: annual averages mask persistent winter severity.

## 10.6 Five-City Seasonal Validation

Individual city panels confirm pattern consistency across all five study cities. Training data shown as solid lines, CPCB 2024 as dashed lines, winter months shaded in blue.

All panels show:

- Dashed line (2024) consistently below solid line (training) — systematic AQI reduction
- Both lines follow identical seasonal shape — pattern preserved
- Winter shading corresponds to highest AQI region on both lines — seasonal learning valid

## 10.7 Validation Summary

| Validation Check                      | Result      |
| ------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| Seasonal pattern preserved (5 cities) | ✓ 5/5       |
| Winter > Monsoon confirmed (5 cities) | ✓ 5/5       |
| SHAP pollutant dominance confirmed    | ✓ 5/5       |
| City pollution ranking preserved      | ✓ YES       |
| Generalization beyond training window | ✓ CONFIRMED |

**Limitation — Systematic AQI Reduction:**
All cities show 13–37% lower AQI in 2024 vs 2015–2020 baseline. Model trained on the older baseline will systematically overpredict current AQI. This does not invalidate the model's learned relationships — seasonal patterns and pollutant dominance are preserved — but absolute predictions will trend high for post-2020 queries. Retraining on 2021–2024 data is recommended before deployment.
